The math on outreach:
Standard "spray and pray" nets a 1-2% reply rate. Tailored emails hit 8-12%. Referrals jump to 35%+. A solid follow-up structure adds a free 3-5% bump. Focus on quality over volume.
Personal Playbook
My internal notes for keeping outreach sharp and effective.
My Email Framework
The Subject: Keep it short (under 50 chars). State the exact role and my core tech stack so they know exactly what the email is about.
The Opener: Don't waste time on pleasantries. One strong sentence about my best recent technical achievement.
The Hook: Show I actually looked into them. Mention a recent launch, engineering post, or specific team expansion.
The Ask: Make it a low-friction request. "Open to a 10-min chat next week?" is easier to say yes to than asking for a formal interview.
The Sign-off: Clean and professional. Name and LinkedIn link. No bulky attachments unless requested.
Follow-Up Cadence
Day 0: The Initial Pitch
Send the full structured email. Keep it between 40-75 words.
Day 5–7: Gentle Nudge
A quick 2-line reply to my own email. Send mid-week, mid-morning.
Day 14: The Breakup
Polite exit. Acknowledge they are busy, keep the door open for the future.
Day 90+: Refresh
If they are still hiring, it's safe to reach out from a new angle.
Mistakes I Need to Avoid
Sending generic copy-paste templates to 100 people.
Opening with "Dear Sir/Madam" or "I am highly passionate...".
Writing an essay. Anything over 150 words won't get read.
Following up after just 48 hours (feels way too desperate).
Emailing 5 different people at the exact same small company.
Timing Rules
Send Days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday.
Send Times: 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM local time.
Avoid Monday mornings (inbox clearing mode).
Avoid Friday afternoons (checked out for the weekend).
Reminder: 10 highly researched, targeted emails will consistently yield better results than 100 generic blasts.
